Early years of the band

Red Hot Chili Peppers (also known as Chili Peppers, RHCP or Red Hot) is an American rock band formed in Los Angeles, California, in 1983.

The Red Hot Chili Peppers have roots since 1979, in the intervals between classes at Fairfax High School in Hollywood, Los Angeles. The boys from 15 years old, Flea, Hillel Slovak and Jack Irons were three friends who had some musical ambitions forming the band called Anthym. One of the great admirers of this band was Anthony Kiedis, also a childhood friend of Flea, Hillel and Irons.

In December 1982, the band Gary and Neighbor's Voices would play at the club Rhythm Lounge in Los Angeles and lead singer Gary Allen suggested that Anthony Kiedis, together with Flea and Hillel Slovak, they opened the show for the band. They then founded a band called "Tony Flow and the Miraculous Masters of Mayhem", because according to Anthony: "that's how we wanted to play, majestic and chaotic".

The group's musical style was heavily influenced by the burgeoning punk scene in Los Angeles (from bands like The Germs, Black Flag, Fear, Minutemen, X) and funk (Parliament-Funkadelic and Sly & the Family Stone).
